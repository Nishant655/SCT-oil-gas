Oil and Gas Wastewater Treatment

Wastewater management in the oil and gas industry is one of the most demanding areas of industrial wastewater treatment. Every stage of upstream, midstream, and downstream operations produces complex wastewater streams that mix hydrocarbons, chemicals, solids, and salts. If not treated properly, these contaminants can harm equipment, increase operational downtime, and impact environmental compliance.

At SCT, we design engineering-driven oil & gas wastewater treatment systems that help refineries, petrochemical units, drilling sites, and processing facilities handle high-strength wastewater with confidence. Our produced water treatment solutions focus on reliability, efficiency, and long-term sustainability, ensuring that your plant meets discharge norms while minimizing operational costs.

Characteristics of Wastewater in the Oil and Gas Industry

Characteristic What does it contain? Where It Comes From / Problems It Causes
Hydrocarbons & Oils Lots of oils, greases, and sticky petroleum bits (like BTEX and PAHs) that don't break down easily. From crude oil pulling and refining; creates oily surface films and harms wildlife.
Suspended Solids (TSS) Tiny dirt particles, clays, silica, calcium precipitates, organic matter, asphaltenes, and bacteria (levels up to 1100 mg/L.) Drilling mud and separators; clogs pipes and settles as sludge.
Dissolved Salts High salinity from chlorides, sulfates, bicarbonates, and more. TDS often exceed seawater levels. Deep underground water; blocks normal treatment and rusts equipment.
Heavy Metals Small amounts of toxic metals like lead, zinc, chromium, nickel, iron, and some radioactive metals. Rusty pipes and added chemicals; pollutes soil/water and affects environment and health long-term.
Organic Chemicals Dissolved organics including phenols, naphthenic acids, VOCs, and sulfides (H2S). Treatment chemicals and oil breaking; raises pollution levels (BOD/COD) and toxicity.
Bacteria & Gases Biofilms, sulfides (FeS), dissolved CO2, mercaptans; promotes souring. Bacteria growing in oil reservoirs; generates odors and corrosion.

Key Challenges in Treating Oil & Gas Wastewater

Oil and gas wastewater is difficult to treat primarily because of its composition and the operational environment. Companies often face challenges such as:

1. High Hydrocarbon Content
Removing free, dispersed, and emulsified oil requires multi-stage separation. Emulsified oil, in particular, demands advanced technologies such as Dissolved Air Flotation (DAF), membrane systems, or coalescers.

2. High TDS and Salinity
Produced water can contain TDS levels exceeding 100,000 mg/L. Conventional biological treatment cannot handle extreme salinity, making thermal or membrane solutions necessary.

3. Variable Flow and Load
Flow rates and pollutant loads can change daily. Treatment systems must stay stable and efficient even under fluctuating conditions.

4. Presence of Toxic Compounds
BTEX, phenols, and heavy metals can inhibit biological systems and require oxidation or specialized filtration.

5. Strict Environmental Regulations
Refineries and E&P operators must comply with stringent discharge standards. Failure to meet norms leads to penalties, downtime, and reputational risks.

6. High OPEX in Traditional Systems
Older plants often consume high energy, rely heavily on chemicals, and require frequent maintenance.

Considering these challenges, choosing the right oil & gas wastewater treatment solution is critical for long-term reliability.

Products for Upstream Wastewater Treatment in the Oil & Gas Industry

Oil & gas operations generate complex wastewater with oil, solids, high COD, and dissolved salts. Advanced treatment technologies are essential to ensure safe discharge, reuse, and regulatory compliance across upstream as well as midstream and downstream facilities.

1. Solid-Liquid Hydrocyclone
The Solid-Liquid Hydrocyclone clarifies solids down to 5 microns using centrifugal force. Its modular, no-backwash design minimizes the footprint and ensures smooth operations in harsh oil & gas conditions. Continuous discharge keeps processes running effectively and efficiently.

2. Liquid-Liquid Hydrocyclone
The Liquid-Liquid Hydrocyclone spins wastewater at up to 4,000 Gs to separate oil from water without chemicals or moving parts. With twin inlets for stabilized flow, it achieves high efficiency and turndown ratios, offering a compact design that maximizes uptime in oil & gas separation.

3. API Separator
The API Separator relies on gravity to skim free oil from wastewater, with vapor covers for odor control and safety. It features double containment and corrosion-resistant parts for leak detection and durability. This system is ideal for primary treatment in oil & gas operations and includes nitrogen blanketing for added safety.

4. Brise™ Dissolved Gas Flotation / Induced Gas Flotation Separators
Brise™ DGF/IGF Separators efficiently separate oil and suspended solids using micro-bubbles to float contaminants to the surface for easy removal. Its high removal efficiency and compact design make it an ideal solution for treating emulsified oils in oil & gas wastewater.

5. Combosep™ System
The Combosep™ System integrates multiple vertical and horizontal separation technologies in one compact unit to remove oils, solids, and other contaminants. It combines gravity separation with flotation, achieving optimal treatment of wastewater with varying flow rates and high efficiency in space-constrained environments.

6. Quadricell Induced Air Flotation/ Induced Gas Flotation Separators
The Quadricell system combines induced air and gas flotation technologies for highly efficient removal of oils and suspended solids. With superior performance in high-throughput conditions, this system is ideal for treating produced water in oil & gas facilities, offering reliable operation and easy maintenance.

7. Veirsep™ Horizontal Flotation System
The Veirsep™ Horizontal Flotation System uses a horizontal flotation vessel to remove oils and solids from wastewater. Its compact design reduces the footprint while maintaining high separation efficiency. This system is particularly effective for high-flow oil & gas wastewater applications.

8. SPINSEP™ Vertical Flotation System
The SPINSEP™ system uses innovative methods within a compact vertical vessel to skim oil from produced water. The ASME-optional build reduces footprint for discharge or injection preparation. It’s an effective and innovative solution for oil & gas wastewater polishing.

9. Auto-Shell™ Filters
This patented design uses shared scrubbing pumps for multi-cell efficiency in oil removal. It eliminates the need for retention screens, simplifying setup and reducing costs. The advanced technology ensures enhanced performance for oil & gas filtration systems.

10. Monosep™ High-flow Walnut Shell Filtration
Monosep™ offers high-flow filtration with no moving parts, backwashing with minimal water use compared to standard designs. Its lightweight, compact footprint reduces installation costs for multi-filter oil & gas systems, providing effective removal of oily contaminants.

The use of technology may vary depending on the quality of the produced wastewater. However, these technologies work together to help oil & gas facilities treat wastewater efficiently, meet regulatory standards, and move closer to sustainable water reuse.

Considerations While Choosing the Best Oil & Gas Wastewater Treatment Solution

Choosing the right wastewater treatment for refineries can significantly reduce your operational burden and long-term costs. Here’s what every oil and gas company should evaluate:

1. Understanding Type and Quality of Wastewater
Produced water demands high-efficiency oil removal and advanced salinity management. Refinery wastewater often requires multi-stage treatment, including biological and polishing steps.

2. Required Treatment Standards
Depending on the discharge requirement, such as surface disposal, reuse, reinjection, or achieving ZLD, the treatment approach must change.

3. Variability of Flow and Load
A system must stay stable even when influent quality fluctuates. Modular and automated solutions work best in such environments.

4. Technology Selection
A good solution will combine mechanical, chemical, biological, and membrane-based processes. Common technologies include Dissolved Air Flotation (DAF), SBR/MBR systems, Advanced Oxidation Processes (AOP), cartridge/multimedia/carbon filters for polishing, and evaporators/crystallizers for ZLD.

5. Energy and Chemical Consumption
Operational costs can quickly escalate if the system is not optimized. Low-energy and low-chemical designs reduce overall OPEX.

6. Automation and Monitoring
SCADA and IoT-based monitoring ensure consistent discharge quality and early detection of abnormalities.

7. Installation Footprint
Oil & gas facilities often have space limitations. Compact, skid-mounted systems offer faster installation and minimal disruption.

8. Long-Term Maintainability
Easy access for maintenance, availability of spares, and trained service support make a big difference in plant reliability.
